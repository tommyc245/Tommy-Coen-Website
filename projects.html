2025 FRC Robotics

Leviathan

BreakerBots 5104 — FIRST Robotics Competition, Reefscape

Photo: Leviathan

Leviathan is a fully autonomous FRC robot capable of scoring on all four levels of the reef. With 4 localization cameras, a ZED stereo camera, and 5 coprocessors, it can operate almost entirely without human input — even during teleop.

Key Systems

  • Vision & Localization — Real-time SLAM on Nvidia Jetson with 4x global shutter cameras on OPi 5 Pro coprocessors. Custom CUDA-accelerated stereo depth pipeline for 3D game piece tracking.
  • Elevator — Custom 3-stage cascade rigged elevator, 80" extension in under 1 second. Counter-balanced with constant force springs, 8 lb effective driven mass. 3D-printed gears.
  • Automation — Single-button scoring on any reef node, autonomous intake via stereo depth, full climb automation. Obstacle avoidance and motion path planning.
  • Climb — Polycarbonate grappling-hook with winch and spring deployment, mechanical lock release for foot extension.

Awards

  • Innovation in Control Award — Contra Costa Regional
  • Industrial Design Award — NorCal Invitational
  • Judges' Award — Pinnacles Regional
  • Gracious Professionalism — San Francisco Regional

2024 FRC Robotics

Typhoon

BreakerBots 5104 — FIRST Robotics Competition, Crescendo

Photo: Typhoon

One of the team's most advanced and automated robots. Typhoon uses intelligent two-button teleop controls — the robot decides what action to perform on its own — along with ML-powered note tracking and multi-note autonomous routines.

Key Systems

  • Shooter — PIDF-controlled pivot on 9:1 reduction with 3D-printed herringbone rack and pinion. Pre-tuned firing solution table for scoring from anywhere on the field.
  • Vision — 4x Arducam OV9281 on 2x OrangePi 5 running PhotonVision, fed to BreakerLib pose filtering and 200Hz multi-threaded Kalman odometry fusion. ML note pursuit via Limelight 3 with Google Coral.
  • Autonomous — 1-5 note autos with AD* replanning and AI note tracking/pursuit.
  • Drivetrain — SDS MK4i L3 swerve with Kraken drive motors.

Awards

  • Regional Finalists — Monterey Bay Regional

2023 FRC Robotics

Kraken / Cthulhu / Plop

BreakerBots 5104 — Charged Up season + off-season development

Photo: 2023 Robots

The 2023 season saw three distinct robots. Kraken — the team's first competition swerve drive robot — featured a double-jointed arm with gas-shock suspension. Cthulhu was designed and built in two weeks between regionals as an evolved design. Plop served as an off-season development platform for next-gen technologies.

Highlights

  • First competition swerve drive robot for BreakerBots
  • Quad-camera vision system with per-camera OrangePi coprocessors
  • Custom BreakerLib pose filtering and odometry fusion
  • Regional Finalists at Central Valley Regional

AtomicHabits App

Personal project — React / TypeScript / Vite

Screenshot: AtomicHabits

A custom-built habit and task tracker inspired by James Clear's Atomic Habits framework. Features repeating tasks with "never miss twice" indicators, drag-and-drop reordering, week-view navigation, reminder system, and a stats page with completion rate analytics.

Technical Details

  • React + TypeScript + Vite with localStorage persistence
  • Natural language date parsing via chrono-node
  • Repeat task system generating 365 future instances
  • Drag-and-drop with order propagation across repeat groups
  • Stats engine with normalized habit names and configurable thresholds
